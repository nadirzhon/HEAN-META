- **Approach:** Thousands of short-term positions (long and short) simultaneously

- **Team Composition:** 250+ PhDs, heavy emphasis on mathematics, physics, cryptography

- Non-compete and non-disclosure agreements for all researchers
- Strategies are closely guarded (even academic papers are restricted)
- "Black box" reputation – externals have limited insight

### Strengths
1. Unmatched quantitative talent (best mathematical minds in finance)
2. Decades of proprietary data and models
3. Infrastructure allowing massive leverage without blowing up
4. No external investor pressure (closed fund)
5. Track record proving the approach works

## 7. ALAMEDA RESEARCH (LESSONS FROM FAILURE)

### Overview (Pre-Collapse)
- **Type:** Crypto trading firm and market maker (sister company to FTX)
- **Peak AUM:** $14.6 billion in assets (November 2022)
- **Collapse:** November 2022 (bankruptcy, $8B+ customer funds lost)

### What Went Wrong

#### 7.1 Commingling of Funds
- **Fatal Flaw:** FTX customer deposits used for Alameda trading
- **Risk Management Failure:** No separation between exchange and trading firm
- **Leverage:** Excessive leverage on illiquid tokens (FTT, SRM)

#### 7.2 Concentration Risk
- **40% of Balance Sheet:** Held in FTT (FTX's own token)
- **Circular Dependency:** FTX issued FTT, Alameda held it as collateral
- **Collapse Trigger:** CoinDesk article exposed this; market lost confidence

#### 7.3 Weak Controls
- No automated risk monitoring that worked
- Manual overrides allowed unlimited withdrawals
- No independent auditing

### Critical Lessons for HEAN

#### 7.1 Avoid Circular Dependencies
**Rule:** Never hold exchange-issued tokens as primary assets
- **HEAN Implementation:** BTC, ETH, SOL only (no CEX utility tokens)

#### 7.2 Implement Real Killswitches
**Alameda's Failure:** "24/7 risk monitoring" was bypassed manually
- **HEAN Solution:** KillSwitch triggers automatically on >20% drawdown (no manual override)

#### 7.3 Diversification is Non-Negotiable
**Alameda's Mistake:** 40% in one token (FTT)
- **HEAN Rule:** Max 20% in any single asset, max 10% in any single position

#### 7.4 Separation of Concerns
**Alameda's Flaw:** Owned by same people as FTX (conflict of interest)
- **HEAN Advantage:** Independent trading system, no exchange ownership
